# Google Local Pack Ranking Factors

Based on industry research and local SEO best practices, these are the primary factors that influence Google Local Pack (map pack) and local organic rankings.

## Ranking Factor Categories and Weights

| Category | Weight | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| GBP Signals | 32% | Google Business Profile optimization |
| On-Page Signals | 19% | Website local optimization |
| Review Signals | 16% | Review quantity, quality, and engagement |
| Link Signals | 11% | Backlink profile with local relevance |
| Behavioral Signals | 8% | User engagement and interaction patterns |
| Citation Signals | 7% | NAP consistency and directory presence |
| Personalization | 7% | Searcher location and history |

## 1. GBP Signals (32%)

The most influential category for local pack rankings.

### Key Factors
- **Proximity to searcher:** The physical distance between the business and the person searching. This is the single most powerful factor and cannot be directly optimized.
- **Primary category relevance:** How well the primary category matches the search query. Choose the most specific category available.
- **Keyword in business name:** Businesses with keywords in their name rank better (do not add keywords artificially - this violates Google guidelines).
- **Secondary categories:** Additional categories expand the queries you can appear for.
- **Address in city of search:** Being physically located in the city being searched matters.
- **GBP completeness:** Fully completed profiles outperform incomplete ones.
- **Google Posts activity:** Regular posting signals an active business.
- **Photo quantity and recency:** Fresh, abundant photos improve engagement signals.

### Optimization Actions
1. Select the most specific primary category
2. Add all relevant secondary categories (up to 9)
3. Complete every available field in your GBP
4. Post at least weekly with images and CTAs
5. Upload new photos regularly (aim for 5+ per month)
6. Keep hours and special hours accurate

## 2. On-Page Signals (19%)

Website-level factors that reinforce local relevance.

### Key Factors
- **NAP on website:** Name, address, phone on every page (footer recommended)
- **Local keywords in title tags:** Include city and state in page titles
- **Local keywords in H1 tags:** Location-relevant headings
- **Local keywords in content:** Natural mentions of service areas
- **Dedicated location pages:** Unique content for each location served
- **Schema markup:** LocalBusiness structured data on the website
- **Mobile-friendly design:** Essential for local searchers on phones
- **Page load speed:** Fast-loading pages rank better
- **Domain authority:** Overall site strength affects local rankings

### Optimization Actions
1. Add consistent NAP to website footer
2. Create unique location pages for each service area
3. Include city + service keyword combinations in title tags
4. Implement LocalBusiness schema markup
5. Ensure mobile responsiveness
6. Optimize page load speed (target under 3 seconds)

## 3. Review Signals (16%)

Reviews directly impact both rankings and conversion.

### Key Factors
- **Review quantity:** Total number of Google reviews
- **Review velocity:** Rate of new reviews over time (steady is better than bursts)
- **Review diversity:** Reviews across multiple platforms (Google, Yelp, Facebook, industry sites)
- **Review keywords:** Reviews that mention services and locations naturally
- **Average star rating:** Higher ratings improve click-through rate
- **Review recency:** Recent reviews carry more weight
- **Owner response rate:** Responding to reviews shows engagement
- **Response quality and timeliness:** Thoughtful, prompt responses matter

### Benchmarks by Industry
| Industry | Median Reviews | Target Rating |
|----------|---------------|---------------|
| Restaurants | 100+ | 4.0+ |
| Home Services | 30+ | 4.5+ |
| Healthcare | 25+ | 4.2+ |
| Legal | 20+ | 4.5+ |
| Retail | 15+ | 4.0+ |
| Professional Services | 15+ | 4.5+ |

### Optimization Actions
1. Implement a review generation process (ask after positive experiences)
2. Respond to every review within 24-48 hours
3. Address negative reviews professionally and constructively
4. Never buy reviews or offer incentives for reviews
5. Create review links and make the process easy for customers
6. Diversify review platforms beyond just Google

## 4. Link Signals (11%)

Backlinks with local relevance boost local rankings.

### Key Factors
- **Local link sources:** Links from other local businesses, organizations, news sites
- **Industry-relevant links:** Links from industry directories and associations
- **Domain authority of linking sites:** Higher authority links carry more weight
- **Anchor text relevance:** Natural anchor text with location or service keywords
- **Link diversity:** Links from many different domains
- **Inbound linking domain authority:** Quality over quantity

### Optimization Actions
1. Join local business associations and chambers of commerce
2. Sponsor local events and organizations
3. Get listed in industry-specific directories
4. Create locally relevant content that earns natural links
5. Build relationships with local bloggers and news outlets
6. Participate in local community events for press coverage

## 5. Behavioral Signals (8%)

How users interact with your listing and website.

### Key Factors
- **Click-through rate (CTR):** Percentage of searchers who click your listing
- **Click-to-call:** Phone calls from the listing
- **Direction requests:** Navigation requests to your location
- **Check-ins:** Users marking themselves at your location
- **Dwell time:** Time spent on your website after clicking
- **Bounce rate:** Users who leave quickly (lower is better)

### Optimization Actions
1. Write compelling GBP descriptions that encourage clicks
2. Use attractive, high-quality photos
3. Keep review ratings high to improve CTR
4. Ensure website delivers on the promise of the listing
5. Provide clear calls to action on landing pages

## 6. Citation Signals (7%)

Directory listings and NAP consistency across the web.

### Key Factors
- **NAP consistency:** Same name, address, phone everywhere
- **Citation volume:** Number of directory listings
- **Citation quality:** Listings on authoritative directories
- **IYP/aggregator signals:** Data from major aggregators
- **Structured vs. unstructured citations:** Both matter

### Optimization Actions
1. Audit existing citations for consistency
2. Fix any NAP inconsistencies found
3. Submit to top-tier directories first
4. Use data aggregators to distribute consistent data
5. Monitor for duplicate listings and merge or remove them

## 7. Personalization (7%)

Factors specific to the individual searcher.

### Key Factors
- **Searcher location:** Physical proximity to the business
- **Search history:** Previous interactions with the business or similar businesses
- **Device type:** Mobile vs. desktop can affect results
- **Time of day:** Business hours and relevance can shift results

### Note
Personalization is largely outside your control. Focus on the factors you can influence to maximize your baseline ranking position.

## Priority Matrix

### High Impact, Low Effort (Do First)
- Complete GBP profile (all fields)
- Set accurate categories
- Respond to all reviews
- Add NAP to website footer
- Implement LocalBusiness schema

### High Impact, High Effort (Plan For)
- Build review generation system
- Create location-specific pages
- Earn local backlinks
- Develop ongoing content strategy

### Lower Impact, Low Effort (Quick Wins)
- Upload fresh photos monthly
- Post weekly on Google Posts
- Pre-populate Q&A section
- Update special hours for holidays

### Lower Impact, High Effort (Consider Later)
- Citation building beyond top 20
- Advanced schema implementations
- Local PR campaigns
