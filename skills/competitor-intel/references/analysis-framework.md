# Competitive Analysis Framework

A structured framework for analyzing SaaS competitors across five dimensions: Product, Pricing, Marketing, Brand, and Traction.

## 1. Product Analysis

Evaluate the competitor's product offering in depth.

### Features

- **Core features:** What are the primary capabilities that define the product?
- **Unique features:** What does this competitor offer that others do not?
- **Missing features:** What notable capabilities are absent compared to alternatives?
- **Feature depth vs breadth:** Is the product a specialist (deep in one area) or generalist (broad but shallow)?

### User Experience

- **Onboarding flow:** How easy is it for a new user to get started and reach first value?
- **Interface design:** Is the UI modern, intuitive, and well-organized?
- **Learning curve:** How much training or documentation is needed to become proficient?
- **Mobile experience:** Is there a mobile app or responsive design? How does it compare to desktop?

### Platform and Infrastructure

- **Deployment model:** Cloud-only, self-hosted, or hybrid?
- **API availability:** Is there a public API? How comprehensive is it?
- **Security certifications:** SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR compliance, HIPAA (if relevant)?
- **Uptime and reliability:** Published SLA? Status page? Historical uptime?

### Integrations

- **Native integrations:** Which tools does the product connect with out of the box?
- **Integration marketplace:** Is there an app store or marketplace for third-party integrations?
- **API/webhook support:** Can customers build custom integrations?
- **Key integration gaps:** Which popular tools are missing from the integration list?

## 2. Pricing Analysis

Understand how the competitor monetizes and how their pricing strategy positions them.

### Pricing Model

- **Model type:** Flat rate, per-seat, usage-based, hybrid, freemium, or reverse trial?
- **Billing options:** Monthly, annual, or both? What discount is offered for annual?
- **Currency and regional pricing:** Do they offer localized pricing?

### Tier Structure

- **Number of tiers:** How many pricing levels are offered?
- **Entry price:** What is the lowest paid tier?
- **Price range:** What is the span from cheapest to most expensive tier?
- **Feature gating:** What features are restricted to higher tiers?
- **Free tier/trial:** Is there a free tier, free trial, or reverse trial? What are the limits?

### Discounts and Incentives

- **Annual discount:** Percentage off for annual commitment?
- **Startup program:** Special pricing for early-stage companies?
- **Nonprofit/education discount:** Reduced pricing for qualified organizations?
- **Volume discounts:** Price breaks at certain seat or usage thresholds?

### Enterprise Pricing

- **Custom pricing:** Is enterprise pricing negotiable and unlisted?
- **Minimum contract:** Is there a minimum annual commitment?
- **Professional services:** Are implementation, training, or consulting services offered?

## 3. Marketing Analysis

Evaluate the competitor's go-to-market strategy and marketing execution.

### Channels

- **Organic search (SEO):** What is their estimated organic traffic? Which keywords do they rank for?
- **Paid search (SEM):** Are they running Google Ads? On what keywords?
- **Social media:** Which platforms are they active on? Follower counts and engagement levels?
- **Content marketing:** Blog frequency, content quality, topic coverage?
- **Email marketing:** Newsletter presence? Nurture sequences?
- **Community:** Do they have a user community, forum, or Slack/Discord group?
- **Events:** Do they host or sponsor webinars, conferences, or meetups?
- **Partnerships:** Channel partners, referral programs, affiliate programs?

### Content Strategy

- **Blog cadence:** How often do they publish? What topics do they cover?
- **Content types:** Blog posts, whitepapers, case studies, videos, podcasts, webinars?
- **SEO focus:** Are they targeting informational, navigational, or transactional keywords?
- **Gated vs ungated:** How much content requires an email to access?
- **Quality assessment:** Is the content original and insightful, or generic and shallow?

### SEO Footprint

- **Domain authority:** Estimated domain authority or rating?
- **Indexed pages:** Approximate number of indexed pages?
- **Top ranking keywords:** What terms drive the most organic traffic?
- **Backlink profile:** Approximate number of referring domains?
- **Technical SEO:** Site speed, mobile-friendliness, structured data usage?

### Social Presence

- **Platform activity:** Which platforms get the most attention (LinkedIn, Twitter/X, YouTube, etc.)?
- **Content style:** Educational, promotional, community-driven, or thought leadership?
- **Engagement rate:** Do posts generate meaningful engagement or is it mostly broadcast?
- **Founder/team presence:** Are executives active on social media as personal brands?

## 4. Brand Analysis

Assess how the competitor positions itself and resonates with its target audience.

### Positioning

- **Positioning statement:** How do they describe themselves in one sentence?
- **Category ownership:** Are they trying to create or own a category?
- **Key differentiator:** What is their primary claim to differentiation?
- **Positioning evolution:** Has their positioning changed over time?

### Messaging

- **Homepage headline:** What is the primary value proposition on the homepage?
- **Messaging pillars:** What 3-5 themes appear consistently across their marketing?
- **Tone and voice:** Professional, casual, technical, aspirational?
- **Jargon level:** Do they use industry jargon or plain language?

### Target Audience

- **Primary segment:** SMB, mid-market, enterprise, or a mix?
- **Industry focus:** Horizontal (all industries) or vertical (specific sectors)?
- **Buyer persona:** Who is the primary buyer (role, title, department)?
- **User persona:** Who are the daily users (may differ from the buyer)?

## 5. Traction Analysis

Gauge the competitor's market momentum and credibility.

### Reviews and Ratings

- **G2/Capterra rating:** Average star rating and number of reviews?
- **Review trends:** Are recent reviews more positive or negative than older ones?
- **Common praise:** What do reviewers consistently love?
- **Common complaints:** What are the recurring negative themes?

### Traffic and Engagement

- **Estimated monthly visitors:** Approximate unique visitors per month?
- **Traffic trend:** Growing, stable, or declining over the last 12 months?
- **Geographic distribution:** Where does most traffic come from?
- **Top traffic sources:** Direct, organic, paid, social, or referral?

### Funding and Financials

- **Total funding raised:** How much capital has been raised?
- **Last round:** Date, amount, and stage of the most recent funding round?
- **Key investors:** Notable VC firms or strategic investors?
- **Revenue estimates:** Any publicly available revenue data or estimates?

### Team and Hiring

- **Team size:** Approximate headcount?
- **Growth rate:** How fast is the team growing (check careers page)?
- **Key hires:** Notable recent hires that signal strategic direction?
- **Engineering ratio:** What proportion of open roles are engineering vs sales vs marketing?

## Market Landscape Mapping

To create a market landscape map:

1. **Choose two axes** that reveal meaningful differences:
   - Price (low to high)
   - Feature breadth (narrow to broad)
   - Target segment (SMB to enterprise)
   - Specialization (horizontal to vertical)
   - Ease of use (complex to simple)
   - Market maturity (new entrant to established)

2. **Plot each competitor** on the 2x2 grid based on available data

3. **Identify patterns:**
   - Which quadrants are crowded?
   - Which quadrants have white space?
   - Where does your product currently sit?
   - Where do you want to be?

4. **Strategic implications:**
   - Crowded quadrants mean high competition and potential price pressure
   - Empty quadrants may represent opportunity or may indicate lack of demand
   - Moving quadrants requires significant investment in product, pricing, or positioning
