## Actionable Recommendations

### 1. Address Pricing Backlash Transparently (Critical)
The May 2025 AI pricing change is the #1 driver of negative Trustpilot reviews. Consider:
- Restoring a mid-tier AI option for Plus plan users
- Increasing the "Limited Trial" from 20 responses to something meaningful
- Publishing a clear "what you get at each tier" comparison

### 2. Invest in Guided Onboarding (High Impact)
"Notion Paralysis" is the most consistent criticism. Solutions:
- In-app onboarding checklist for new users
- "Start with a template" flow instead of blank page
- Role-based setup wizards ("I'm an engineer" → pre-configured workspace)

### 3. Prioritize Database Performance (High Impact)
Performance at scale is a retention risk for power users and enterprise accounts:
- Publish performance benchmarks and improvement milestones
- Consider pagination or lazy-loading for large databases
- Communicate WASM SQLite improvements more visibly to users

### 4. Expand Offline Capabilities (Medium Impact)
Basic offline since August 2025 is acknowledged but insufficient:
- Roadmap transparency on full offline editing timeline
- Prioritize common offline use cases (viewing, basic editing)

### 5. Reduce AI Intrusiveness (Medium Impact)
Automatic AI pop-ups during calls and meetings frustrate users:
- Make AI suggestions opt-in, not opt-out
- Allow users to disable specific AI triggers
- Separate "AI assistant" from "AI automation" controls

## Executive Summary

**Notion's review sentiment is strong but showing cracks.** The 4.7 G2/Capterra ratings reflect genuine user satisfaction with the platform's flexibility and all-in-one value. However, three emerging risks threaten long-term sentiment health:

1. **Pricing resentment** from the May 2025 AI restructure is creating a vocal negative minority on consumer platforms
2. **Learning curve + performance** remain the most consistent criticisms and have not been adequately addressed
3. **AI reliability** concerns are growing as Notion doubles down on AI-first positioning (Notion 3.0)

The positive signals (flexibility, consolidation, template community, AI when it works) still outweigh the negatives. But the gap between professional-platform ratings (4.7) and consumer-platform ratings (~3.0) suggests a growing satisfaction divide that warrants attention.
